Journey-Autism

My name is Cindy and I would like to share a little part of my son Noah's journey with Autism. Noah is 9 years old and currently in 3rd grade. He is in General Ed with Resource support. He goes to the Resource classroom to receive speech, social, occupational and behavioral therapy (including brushing). He also goes to his Resource classroom when he is becoming overwhelmed in his regular classroom.

It took 2 years to get Noah diagnosed with Autism, specifically PDD-NOS (Pervasive Developmental Disorder- Not Otherwise Specified). This is a super high functioning type of Autism but it is also a very rare type and there is currently not a lot of information about it. After fighting with the school he was attending I took him to the Crystal Cherry Ball Foundation in Dallas, TX and Cook Children's Hospital network in Fort Worth, TX. He was 4 years old when we finally had an official diagnosis.

I knew something was going on but did not know how to help. It broke my heart daily. He would have fits, was nonverbal, would scream without stopping, and it would require me to physically compress him to calm him down. I would sit him on my lap, tightly wrap my arms around him and then wrap my legs over his in "criss-cross applesauce" style. After continued therapies, most specifically brushing therapy, he started to gain control, speaks very well and a lot now, walks on his own, makes eye contact, initiates conversations and recently even started wanting to try to write things in class instead of just verbally provide responses for his work.

He still has some OCD type tendencies that can cause some tension but has continued to gain control of his outbursts and now uses his words to let his teachers and us know when he needs a break. He is allowed to wear earphones at school to help with situations that he feels overwhelmed in due to noise levels. He can also ask to go to his Resource classroom to give himself space and time to calm down. This is a major improvement as before he would scream, cover his ears, run out and even sometimes take off his clothes in frustration. He has also learned to stay on track and get where he is supposed to be going without wandering off by using a timer that he carries between classes to let him know when he is supposed to be at the other classroom.

He has pretty bad allergies to nuts and cow's milk so we control his diet quite a bit. Once we learned this and altered his diet we also noticed that he calmed down. He was having lots of pain and digestive issues that we were unsure of the cause until tests were ran at his GI doctor's office. The combination of controlling his diet, the various therapies, the consistency between home and school, new information we learn and lots of love and patience have contributed to Noah's success in continually growing and functioning more independently and on-level with other kids his age. Don't get me wrong, some days are still a struggle and may always be, but the progress we continue to see and the life lessons we learn from loving him are beautiful parts of our lives that we would never change.
