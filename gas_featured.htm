DFW's Local Music Scene Connection Spot

Featured Bands, Artists and Venues

"This page is the appetizer where we showcase featured bands, artists and venues that have either caught our attention or have been brought to our attention. This month we are featuring our favorite venues on this page."

Lola's is one of our favorite spots! Brian Forella opened Lola's in 2007 and it has become not only a local favorite hot spot but is also a nationally recoginized venue. They host one of the widest varieties of genres around and bands and artists, both local and touring, consider it "home". Lola's has seriously helped cultivate the music scene in Fort Worth by providing a venue that rivals those that would normally be chosen in Dallas instead. We highly recommend that you check out this awesome venue and partake in some serious musical talent as you will find something that everyone will like! Check out our links page. to visit their website.


The Foundry is another of our favorite spots. This is definitely not your usual venue. On one side you have their delicious resturant The Chicken Scratch and then you have bar area on the other. Food, drinks and places to relax and hang out are located inside the shipping containers that have been transformed. There is also a nice outdoor patio area with picnic tables and all. The stage is located outside in the patio area where a wide variety of bands and artist perform on Friday and Saturday nights. You will feel warm and welcome here in the laid back, dog friendly atmosphere. We highly recommend visiting this hidden gem located in Oak Cliff. Check out our links page to visit their website.


1919 Hemphill is a 14 year veteran to the punk scene but they also feature many other genres in an all ages welcome atmosphere. Their motto of "No booze, no drugs, no jerks" keeps this ecletic DIY venue a favorite for people of all ages. It is located in an area of southern Fort Worth that thrives on turning once abandoned buildings into artsy spaces. There are couches and chairs in one area, inspiring graffit on the walls and an in your face stage area that creates an energy that can only be felt and described after visiting. We love this hole in the wall venue and look forward to upcoming shows. Definitely try this place out at least once! Check out our links page to visit their Facebook page.

Fred's Texas Cafe is not only a great place to enjoy live, local music but the food and drinks are phenomenal as well! The outdoor patio area is covered and the stage sits in the center. We love visiting here often for the music, food and ice cold drinks. This is a family friendly venue and a great date spot as well. Definitely check out Fred's! Check out our links page to visit their website.


The Scat Jazz Lounge gives that authentic speak-easy fell. It is located below street level in Sundance Square which adds to the element of the era. You can catch local and national acts throughout the week. The vibe is very grown and sexy but chill and relaxing. We venture here at least once a month. Perfect place to bring a date! Check out our links page to visit their website.


Buttons is definitely one of our favorites as well. The food is delicious (you have to try the made from scratch Chicken&Waffles plate!), the drinks are ice cold with a decent variety, and the live music is superb. This place has a down-home vibe that will warm your heart and soul. This place is a must visit and we frequent as often as possible. Check out our links page to visit their website.


If you are looking for a local venue that is loud and proud to showcase local bands as well as hosting touring bands then look no further than the Rail Club. The Rail is a must visit venue if you want to see and support up-and-coming local rock/punk/metal bands. The stage and accoustics are good and the vibe is definitely chill but crazy all at the same time. We frequent the Rail often and recommend adding it to your list. Check out our links page to visit their website.
